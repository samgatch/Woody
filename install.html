Installs We Provide

Gas

Gas Fireplaces are the most common unit we install. They are efficient and provide a cozy atmosphere to your home. They offer a variety of different styles and sizes for you to choose from. The units we install are all fully contained units. This means, there is no use of air inside your home to fuel the fire, they have a layer of glass and a screen front in between wandering hands and the flame, and there is no draft created when the unit isn't running.

The Pros:

  • No Back Draft
  • The Flame is Out of Reach

The Cons:

  • Not a lot of Heat
  • Requires More Matinence
  • More Tech Can Mean More Issues

Wood Burning

Woodburning Fireplaces are also fairly common of an install. As opposed to gas units woodburning units generally provide a bit more heat, as well as, the crackling sound from burning logs. They do however have a touchable flame and can create a cold draft if the damper is open. They also must have air available in the home in order to burn.

The Pros:

  • More Heat
  • Fire Ambiance
  • No Electric Components

The Cons:

  • Open Fire
  • May Have Draft
  • Manually Feed Fire
  • Clean Ash Out

Insert

Inserts are gas units that are installed into existing masonry or woodburning units. They convert the unit into a gas unit without having to rip out an old unit.

The Pros:

  • Easy Install
  • No Back Draft
  • The Flame is Out of Reach

The Cons:

  • Smaller Burning Area
  • Not a lot of Heat
  • Requires More Matinence
  • More Tech Can Mean More Issues

Gas Logs

Gas Logs, similar to inserts are installed in existing units. They are easy to install and change styles and can be taken out to still burn wood.

The Pros:

  • Easy to Install
  • Fire Ambiance
  • No Electric Components

The Cons:

  • Open Fire
  • May Have Draft
  • Low Heat
  • Requires Matinence

Electric

Electric Fireplaces are the easiest to install, but also provide the least amount of heat. They are used in a more modern setting and for smaller budgets.

The Pros:

  • Easy to Install
  • No Gas or Wood

The Cons:

  • Less Ambiant
  • Only Work With Power
